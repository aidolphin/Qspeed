Frequently Asked Questions

Qspeed uses multi-connection streaming to saturate your bandwidth, similar to professional speed tests. Results are affected by Wi-Fi signal, network devices, and server distance. Connect via Ethernet for best accuracy.

For general browsing, 25 Mbps download is sufficient. Streaming 4K video requires at least 50 Mbps. For gaming, low ping (under 30ms) matters more than raw speed. Remote work with video calls needs 10+ Mbps upload.

Jitter is the variation in ping times. High jitter (above 30ms) causes choppy video calls, stuttering in games, and buffering during streaming. Qspeed measures jitter by analyzing the consistency of ping samples.

Packet loss is the percentage of data packets that fail to reach their destination. Even 1–2% loss can degrade real-time applications like VoIP and gaming. Zero packet loss is ideal.

Qspeed is completely free with no signup required. Your test results are stored locally on your device and are never sent to our servers. The only network requests made are to the speed test server during a test.

ISPs advertise "up to" speeds under ideal conditions. Real-world speeds are reduced by Wi-Fi overhead, distance from the router, network congestion, old wiring, and router quality. Test with Ethernet for the closest match.
